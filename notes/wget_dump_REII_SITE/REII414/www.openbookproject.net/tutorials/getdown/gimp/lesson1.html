Lesson 1: Toolbar

In this first lesson we are going to become familiar with the toolbar.

Here is the toolbar:

All of the cool effects that are available with GIMP are accessed through the toolbar.

We begin with the File button in the top left hand corner of the toolbar. If you left click on the File button, the following will pop up:

Clicking on New will open up a blank image. This will allow you to work from scratch on your own image.

When you click on New, a window will pop up that allows you to set the size of your blank image.

By default, pixels are used as the unit of measure. To get a feel for the size of a pixel, the standard computer desktop these days measures 1024 by 768 pixels. If you wanted to make a background image for your desktop you should make it that size.

The Select Tools

The first part of the toolbar that we are going to explore is the rectangular region tool. The button looks like this...

With this tool, you can create rectangular regions that you can fill with different colors or patterns. Lets try this:

  1. Click on the rectangular region button and move your cursor over to your image.
  2. Click on the image and hold the mouse button down.
  3. Move the mouse until you have a rectangle of your liking and release the button.

This process of holding the mouse button and dragging the pointer is usually called clicking and dragging. The region you created will blink, so don't be alarmed. It should look something like this:

You can fill this region with a color to create a colored rectangle box.

  1. Right click with the mouse on the blinking region.
  2. Then move down to the Edit button and select Fill with FG color.
  3. Your rectangle will turn black.

You will now have a black box on your image. If it doesn't look like the image below, try again.

You can also change the color that you fill the boxes with by changing the foreground or background colors that are currently set to black and white. On the main GIMP screen there are 2 boxes (black and white; one on top of the other). Click on the top box and select a different color.

You can do similar things with the elliptical region button. The elliptical region button looks like this:

With this button, you can do exactly the same things as the rectangular region. Just click on the button, move your cursor over to your image, draw your region, and fill the image with a color.

The final drawing tool that we are going to look at in this lesson is the hand drawn region tool. The button for this tool looks like this:

This is an extremely cool tool. You can create any shape you want. To use the tool, just follow along with these steps:

  1. First, click on the hand drawn region tool button.
  2. Then, move your cursor over to your blank image.
  3. Hold down the mouse button.
  4. Drag the cursor around to a point on the image.
  5. Then let go of the mouse button.

The GIMP will automatically draw a line from your original point to the second point where you let go of the mouse button. Here is an example...

As you see, we started out at point A and eventually finished at point B. GIMP then created a straight line connecting these two points to complete the selection.

Alright, this is what the scissors select tool, looks like on the toolbar.

You will notice that this tool can make a strange selection of your choosing. All you have to do is left click on the image and it will make a dot. From that point, everytime you click, another dot will appear. If you click on an existing dot, you can close the selection. Now you can alter the selection, like filling it in with white, as I did in the picture above. You can change the selection by dragging the borders, or by dragging a point that you've placed.
